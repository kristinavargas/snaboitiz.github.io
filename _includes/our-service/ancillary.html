Ancillary Services

To help support the gridâ€™s reliability and stability, we have also been a partner of the National Grid Corporation of the Philippines (NGCP) in providing ancillary services. The types of ancillary service we can provide are regulating reserve, contingency reserve, dispatchable reserve, and black start.

Regulating Reserve (RR)

uses a synchronized generating unit that adjusts real-time with the system demand to maintain the frequency of the system at 60 HZ. It counters the small changes in the system to maintain the balance of supply and demand.

Contingency Reserve (CR)

uses a synchronized generating unit that replaces power plants on forced outages that may cause a supply-demand imbalance.

Dispatchable Reserve (DR)

uses a standby generating unit that replaces CR (see above) to maintain the balance of supply and demand for a longer period after CR has been utilized.

Black Start (BS)

is a service that is used to jump-start the system during a system-wide blackout.
